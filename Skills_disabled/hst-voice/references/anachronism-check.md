# Anachronism Check

Thompson died in February 2005. His working vocabulary is his and his time's. If the piece is set after his death, the thought experiment places him alive in the present, but his idioms would not have absorbed the online and institutional register that solidified after he stopped writing. Use this file as a check before finalising any piece set after 2005.

## Post-2005 idioms to avoid

These phrases mark the prose as written after 2005, not by him:

- "fail upward" / "failing upward" (mid-2010s managerial idiom)
- "subscription service" as a metaphor for anything other than actual subscriptions
- "not normal" as political critique (2016-era resistance register)
- "norm erosion," "democratic backsliding," "guardrails"
- "tote bag" or "NPR tote" as class signifier
- "cope," "seethe," "mald" (online register)
- "bad faith actor" / "acting in bad faith" (legal term weaponised online c. 2015)
- "the discourse" with definite article (2010s academic-online)
- "centring" or "de-centring" as verbs outside geometry
- "hot take"
- "energy" as a quality of a person or mood ("strong dad energy")
- "unprecedented" used for events that are in fact commonplace
- "gaslighting" outside of the 1944 film reference
- "late-stage capitalism" (academic Marxism that escaped the seminar after 2012)
- "billionaire class" (Sanders 2016 onward)
- "the algorithm" as an active agent
- "main character" as a social-media concept
- "touch grass"
- "based," "cringe," "mid" as evaluations
- "It's giving [X]" constructions
- "lived experience" outside phenomenology
- "doing the work"
- "quiet quitting"
- "vibes" as a noun of analysis
- "tech bro," "finance bro," any "-bro" construction

## His equivalents

Where these modern idioms would sit, Thompson reached for different materials. "Bad faith actor" becomes "swine" or "thug" or a named person with a physical slander attached. "Not normal" becomes something bodily: the machine is loose, the fuse is short, we have lost the plot. "Fail upward" becomes a named career trajectory with a named man at the centre and a specific contract at the bottom. "Late-stage capitalism" becomes a specific deal, a specific CEO, a specific refinery, a specific check that did not clear.

The discipline is replacement, not deletion. The modern idiom is trying to do work. Find the pre-2005 equivalent that does the same work in his hand.

## Proper nouns he could plausibly reference

For pieces set in the 2005-2026 window, he could plausibly reference: the September 11 attacks and aftermath (he wrote about these), the Iraq War, George W. Bush, Dick Cheney, Donald Rumsfeld, Karl Rove, Kenneth Starr, most Clinton-era figures, the 2000 election theft, boxing and football figures active before his death. He cannot plausibly reference cultural events or figures that emerged after February 2005 unless the thought-experiment frame makes clear he lived past his actual death and paid attention to what came after.

If the frame is "Thompson alive in 2026," he has had twenty-one years to absorb new names. He would know who Trump is because Trump was already Trump in the 1980s and '90s and Thompson would have been tracking him. He would know JD Vance, Elon Musk, MAGA, ICE, the new war. But he would filter them through a 2005 imagination, not a 2020s one.

## Physical and sensory reach

His similes reached for: firearms, boxing, gambling, cars, dogs, horses, boats, fishing, weather, bar-fights, specific rooms in specific hotels, Kentucky childhood, the 1960s counterculture, the Kennedy assassinations, the press corps, motorcycles, the Hell's Angels.

They did not reach for: software, streaming, social media, venture capital, podcasts, wellness culture, the gig economy, crypto, subscription economics, remote work. A metaphor from his toolkit will read as him. A metaphor from the modern toolkit will read as you.

## The tell most imitators miss

The post-2005 political-criticism register assumes a certain institutional faith the writer is watching erode. Thompson had no such faith to begin with. His 1972 prose already treated the Republic as largely lost. An imitation that mourns the loss of norms sounds like a 2018 op-ed writer; Thompson writing in 2026 would sound like someone who figured the whole game was rigged decades ago and is now watching the specific form the collapse takes. The difference in register is small on the page and large in effect.
